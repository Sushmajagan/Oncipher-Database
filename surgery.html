Surgery

Surgery is often the first line of treatment for breast cancer, aimed at removing cancerous tissue and preventing the disease from spreading. There are several types of surgical procedures, each tailored to the individual patient's condition and preferences.

Types of Breast Cancer Surgery

  • Breast-Conserving Surgery (Lumpectomy)
    • This procedure involves removing the tumor along with a small margin of surrounding healthy tissue. It is also known as a wide local excision. Lumpectomy is often followed by radiation therapy to reduce the risk of recurrence in the same breast.
  • Mastectomy
    • A mastectomy entails the removal of one or both breasts. There are different variations:
    1. Total (Simple) Mastectomy: Removal of the entire breast, including the nipple and areola.
    2. Modified Radical Mastectomy: Removal of the entire breast along with some lymph nodes under the arm.Patients may opt for breast reconstruction after a mastectomy, which can be done immediately or at a later date.
  • Lymph Node Surgery
    • This involves removing lymph nodes to check for cancer spread. Techniques include sentinel lymph node biopsy (removing a few key nodes) and axillary lymph node dissection (removing multiple nodes).
  • Breast Reconstruction
    • After mastectomy, patients may choose to undergo reconstructive surgery to restore the breast's appearance. Options include implants or autologous tissue reconstruction, where tissue from another part of the body is used. Source
