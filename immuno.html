Immunotherapy

Immunotherapy has emerged as a critical and promising approach in breast cancer treatment, particularly for specific subtypes like triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) and HER2-positive cancers.

Monoclonal Antibody Therapies

HER2-Targeted Therapies:

  • Trastuzumab
  • Pertuzumab
  • T-DM1 (ado-trastuzumab emtansine)

Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors

  • Focus on PD-1/PD-L1 blockade
  • Particularly effective in triple-negative breast cancer

Pembrolizumab (Keytruda) showed promising results:

  • Improved overall survival to 23.0 months vs. 16.1 months with chemotherapy alone.

Emerging Approaches

  • Cancer vaccines
  • CAR-T cell therapies
  • Bispecific antibodies
  • Oncolytic viruses

Clinical Significance

Immunotherapy represents a transformative approach in breast cancer treatment, offering:

  • Targeted treatment
  • Potential for long-term immune activation
  • Improved survival rates
  • Options for previously challenging cancer subtypes
