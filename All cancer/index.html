1. Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is one of the most common cancers among women worldwide. It develops in the cells of the breast, often presenting as a lump or abnormal mammogram. Early detection through self-exams and regular screenings significantly improves survival rates.

Key Symptoms: Lump in the breast or underarm, nipple discharge, changes in breast shape/size

Treatment Options: Surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, hormone therapy, targeted therapySource

2. Vulvar Cancer

Vulvar cancer affects the external female genitalia and is relatively rare. It typically develops slowly and may be linked to human papillomavirus (HPV) infection.

Key Symptoms: Itching, burning, pain, or lumps/ulcers in the vulvar area

Treatment Options: Surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapySource

3. Cervical Cancer

Cervical cancer arises in the cervix, the lower part of the uterus. It's strongly associated with HPV infection and can be detected early through routine Pap smears and HPV tests.

Key Symptoms: Abnormal vaginal bleeding, pelvic pain, pain during intercourse

Treatment Options: Surgery, radiation, chemotherapy, immunotherapySource

4. Prostate Cancer

Prostate cancer is one of the most common cancers in men, particularly those over 50. It often grows slowly and may not cause serious harm if detected early.

Key Symptoms: Difficulty urinating, blood in urine/semen, pelvic discomfort

Treatment Options: Active surveillance, surgery, radiation therapy, hormone therapySource

5. Glioblastoma

Key Symptoms: Headaches, seizures, memory loss, personality changes

Treatment Options: Surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted drug therapySource

6. Lip and Oral Cancer

These cancers affect the lips, mouth, tongue, gums, and throat. Tobacco and alcohol use significantly increase risk.

Key Symptoms: Mouth sores that don‚Äôt heal, lumps, difficulty chewing or swallowing

Treatment Options: Surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapySource

7. Bladder Cancer

Bladder cancer typically begins in the lining of the bladder and is more common in older adults. Smoking is a significant risk factor.

Key Symptoms: Blood in urine, frequent urination, pelvic pain

Treatment Options: Surgery, intravesical therapy, chemotherapy, immunotherapySource

8. Colon (Colorectal) Cancer

Colorectal cancer affects the colon or rectum and is preventable with regular screenings like colonoscopies.

Key Symptoms: Changes in bowel habits, blood in stool, unexplained weight loss

Treatment Options: Surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted therapySource

9. Liver Cancer

Liver cancer often develops in people with chronic liver disease, such as hepatitis B/C or cirrhosis.

Key Symptoms: Upper abdominal pain, weight loss, jaundice, fatigue

Treatment Options: Surgery, liver transplant, ablation therapy, targeted therapySource

10. Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is the leading cause of cancer-related deaths globally, with smoking as the major risk factor.

Key Symptoms: Persistent cough, chest pain, shortness of breath, coughing up blood

Treatment Options: Surgery, chemotherapy, radiation, immunotherapy, targeted therapySource

11. Ovarian Cancer

Often called the ‚Äúsilent killer,‚Äù ovarian cancer can be difficult to detect early. It begins in the ovaries and may spread to the pelvis and abdomen.

Key Symptoms: Abdominal bloating, pelvic pain, frequent urination

Treatment Options: Surgery, chemotherapy, targeted therapySource

12. Thyroid Cancer

Thyroid cancer affects the thyroid gland and is usually very treatable. It's more common in women and younger adults.

Key Symptoms: Lump in the neck, voice changes, difficulty swallowing

Treatment Options: Surgery, radioactive iodine therapy, hormone therapySource

13. Non-Hodgkin's Lymphoma

This cancer affects the lymphatic system and includes a wide variety of lymphocyte (white blood cell) cancers.

Key Symptoms: Swollen lymph nodes, fatigue, fever, weight loss

Treatment Options: Chemotherapy, radiation therapy, immunotherapy, stem cell transplantSource

14. Stomach (Gastric) Cancer

Stomach cancer begins in the lining of the stomach and is more common in older individuals.

Key Symptoms: Indigestion, loss of appetite, abdominal pain, nausea

Treatment Options: Surgery, chemotherapy, radiation therapy, targeted therapySource
