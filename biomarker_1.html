SCC Antigen (SCC Ag) lesser 37 U/mL Elevated in 24-90% of cervical cancer patients, depending on stage Indicates presence of squamous cell carcinoma; higher levels correlate with advanced disease stages.

CYFRA 21-1 lesser than 3.3 ng/mL Elevated levels correlate with disease presence and progression More frequently positive in squamous cell carcinoma; decline during treatment indicates response.

Biomarkers of cervical cancer include proteins like SCC antigen and CYFRA 21-1, as well as microRNAs such as miR-21 and miR-210-3p, which are utilized for early detection, monitoring disease progression, and predicting treatment response.
