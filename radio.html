Radiotherapy

Radiation therapy is a critical component in the management of cervical cancer, utilized across various stages of the disease.

Types of Radiation Therapy

1.External Beam Radiation Therapy (EBRT):

  • High-energy x-rays are directed at the tumor from outside the body.
  • Often used as a primary treatment for locally advanced cervical cancer (stages IB3-IVA) and as adjuvant therapy post-surgery for high-risk patients.
  • Typically administered in daily fractions over several weeks, with doses ranging from 4500 to 5040 cGy.

2.Brachytherapy:

  • Involves placing radioactive material directly inside or near the tumor.
  • Commonly used in conjunction with EBRT for definitive treatment, particularly effective in delivering high doses to the cervix while minimizing exposure to surrounding tissues.
  • Can be performed using high-dose-rate (HDR) techniques, often administered 1-2 weeks after external radiation.
