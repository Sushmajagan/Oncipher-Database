Cytogenetics

Page Title

Cervical cancer cytogenetics encompasses the study of chromosomal abnormalities and genetic alterations that underlie the initiation, progression, and heterogeneity of the disease. These changes are closely linked to human papillomavirus (HPV) infection, which plays a central role in cervical carcinogenesis.

Chromosomal Alterations in Cervical Cancer

Nonrandom structural and numerical changes affect multiple chromosomes, as shown by classical cytogenetics and molecular techniques like fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) and comparative genomic hybridization (CGH)

Frequent chromosomal gains include:

1. 3q (harboring TERC and PIK3CA)

2. 5p

3. 8q (notably the MYC oncogene region)

4. 11q22 (including YAP1, BIRC2, BIRC3)

5. 7p11.2 (containing EGFR)

Common chromosomal losses occur at:

1. 3p

2. 4q35.2 (FAT1)

3. 10q23.31 (PTEN)

4. 11q

5. 18q21.2 (SMAD4)Source
