Imaging tests play a crucial role in the diagnosis, staging, and management of cervical cancer.

1. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

Purpose:

MRI is the preferred imaging method for assessing local cervical cancer. It provides detailed images of soft tissues and is particularly useful for evaluating tumor size, parametrial invasion, and involvement of adjacent structures such as the bladder and rectum.

Advantages:
  • High sensitivity for detecting local tumor spread.
  • Non-invasive with no exposure to ionizing radiation.

Limitations

 MRI may not be readily available in all settings and can be more expensive than other imaging modalities.

2. Computed Tomography (CT) Scan

Purpose:

 CT scans are commonly used to evaluate extrauterine spread of cervical cancer and to assess lymph node involvement.

Advantages:
  • Provides a comprehensive view of the abdomen and pelvis.
  • Useful for identifying metastasis to distant organs.

Limitations
  • Lower sensitivity for local disease compared to MRI.
  • Cannot reliably differentiate between normal cervical tissue and cancerous tissue.
