Genes associated in Breast Cancer

S.No. Gene Type Molecular Function Significance / Inference Reference
1. HER2 Oncogene Transmembrane tyrosine kinase receptor Driver of HER2+ breast cancer; targeted by therapies like trastuzumab (1)
2. BRCA1 Tumor suppressor DNA repair via homologous recombination Mutations increase hereditary breast cancer risk; linked to genomic instability (1)(2)
3. RAD51 DNA repair gene Homologous recombination repair Overexpression in breast cancer correlates with poor prognosis (2)
4. CCNB1 Cell cycle regulator G2/M phase transition (cyclin B1) Upregulated in tumors; associated with aggressive subtypes and shorter survival (2)
5. CHEK1 DNA damage checkpoint Cell cycle arrest in response to DNA damage High expression linked to chemotherapy resistance and poor outcomes (2)
6. CDK1 Cell cycle kinase Regulation of mitosis and cell division Overexpression correlates with highest hazard ratio (HR=1.68) for mortality (2)
7. VEGFA Angiogenesis factor Vascular endothelial growth factor signaling Promotes tumor angiogenesis and metastasis; potential anti-angiogenic therapy target (2)
8. XRCC4 DNA repair gene Non-homologous end-joining repair Elevated expression in tumors; HR=1.25 for mortality (2)
9. TP53 Tumor suppressor Cell cycle control and apoptosis Mutated in ~30% of breast cancers; drives genomic instability (1)
10. PTEN Tumor suppressor PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway inhibition Loss of function linked to therapy resistance and poor prognosis (1)
