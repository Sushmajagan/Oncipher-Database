The diagnosis of cervical cancer involves a series of steps and tests aimed at detecting abnormalities in the cervix, confirming the presence of cancer, and determining its stage.

Clinical Examination Pap Smear (Papanicolaou Test):

This is the primary screening tool used to detect precancerous changes and cervical cancer. During this test, cells are collected from the cervix using a brush or spatula and sent to a laboratory for cytological examination. Abnormal results may indicate the presence of dysplasia or cancerous cells.

HPV Testing:

Often performed alongside the Pap smear, this test detects high-risk types of human papillomavirus (HPV) that are associated with cervical cancer. The sample collected for the Pap test can usually be used for HPV testing as well.
