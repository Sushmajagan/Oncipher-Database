Magnetic resonance imaging

Breast magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is a vital imaging modality used in the diagnosis, staging, and management of breast cancer. It leverages powerful magnets and radio waves to create detailed images of breast tissue, providing critical information that complements other imaging techniques such as mammography and ultrasound.

Imaging Characteristics Lesion Features 1.Malignant Lesions: On MRI, malignant tumors often appear as irregularly shaped hypoechoic masses with spiculated margins. They may also exhibit enhancement patterns that suggest malignancy. 2.Benign Lesions: Benign tumors typically have well-defined borders and a more homogeneous appearance. They may present as oval or round masses
