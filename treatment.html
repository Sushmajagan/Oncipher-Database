Cervical cancer treatment typically involves a combination of therapies tailored to the stage of cancer, its location, and the patient's overall health.

Sugery

Types of Surgical Procedures:

Cone Biopsy (Conization):

Removal of a cone-shaped section of the cervix, often used for early-stage cancer or precancerous changes.

Radical Trachelectomy:

Removal of the cervix and part of the vagina, preserving the uterus for potential future pregnancies, suitable for early-stage cervical cancer.

Hysterectomy:

Complete removal of the uterus and cervix, which may include removal of surrounding tissues and lymph nodes. This is often recommended for more advanced cancers.

Pelvic Exenteration:

In cases where cervical cancer has spread locally, this extensive surgery involves removing the cervix, uterus, vagina, bladder, and possibly parts of the bowel. Source
