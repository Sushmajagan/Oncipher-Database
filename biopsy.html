Biopsy

A biopsy is the definitive method for diagnosing breast cancer. It involves removing tissue or fluid from the breast to be examined under a microscope. There are several types of biopsies:

  • Fine-Needle Aspiration: A thin needle is used to extract cells.
  • Core Biopsy: A larger needle removes a core sample of tissue.
  • Open Biopsy: A surgical procedure to remove a larger area of tissue
