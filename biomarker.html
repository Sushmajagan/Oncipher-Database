Biomarker of Cervical Cancer

Biomarker Normal Range/Expression Abnormal Measures Significance Reference
SCC Antigen (SCC Ag) lesser 37 U/mL Elevated in 24-90% of cervical cancer patients, depending on stage Indicates presence of squamous cell carcinoma; higher levels correlate with advanced disease stages. (1)(2)(3)
CA19-9 lesser than 37 U/mL Elevated levels may indicate malignancy, including cervical cancer Associated with tumor burden; not specific but can aid in monitoring treatment response. (1)(2)
CYFRA 21-1 lesser than 3.3 ng/mL Elevated levels correlate with disease presence and progression More frequently positive in squamous cell carcinoma; decline during treatment indicates response. (4)(5)(6)
miRNA-29a Variable (baseline not well-defined) Elevated in early-stage cervical cancer Part of a multi-biomarker panel for non-invasive detection; high sensitivity and specificity. (1)(3)
miRNA-25 Variable (baseline not well-defined) Elevated in early-stage cervical cancer Contributes to the accuracy of multi-biomarker panels for early detection. (1)(3)
miRNA-486-5p Variable (baseline not well-defined) Elevated in early-stage cervical cancer Enhances the sensitivity and specificity of biomarker panels for cervical cancer detection. (1)(3)
