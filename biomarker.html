Biomarker of Breast Cancer

Biomarker Normal Measure Abnormal Measure Clinical Significance Reference
Estrogen Receptor (ER) <1% tumor cells positive by IHC ≥1% tumor cells positive by IHC Positive ER indicates hormone receptor–positive breast cancer; predicts response to endocrine therapy (e.g., tamoxifen, aromatase inhibitors) and better prognosis (1)(2)(3)
Progesterone Receptor (PR) <1% tumor cells positive by IHC >≥1% tumor cells positive by IHC PR positivity often accompanies ER positivity; indicates hormone responsiveness and better prognosis (1)(2)(3)
Ki-67 (Proliferation Index) Low level, typically <14% positive cells High level, typically ≥14% positive cells High Ki-67 indicates high tumor proliferation, worse prognosis, and may guide chemotherapy decisions, especially in Luminal B subtype (1)(3)(4)
CA 15-3 (Cancer Antigen 15-3) <30 U/mL (varies by assay) Elevated (>30 U/mL) Used for monitoring treatment response and disease progression in advanced breast cancer; not diagnostic alone (1)(3)(5)
CA 27.29 <38 U/mL (varies by assay) Elevated (>38 U/mL) Similar to CA 15-3, used for monitoring metastatic breast cancer and treatment response (3)(5)
Carcinoembryonic Antigen (CEA) <5 ng/mL (varies) Elevated (>5 ng/mL) May indicate tumor activity or metastasis; less specific, can be elevated in benign conditions (1)(3)(5)
Bcl-2 Normal expression in cells Overexpression or underexpression Anti-apoptotic protein; expression patterns correlate with tumor subtype and prognosis (3)
p53 Wild-type expression Mutations or overexpression Tumor suppressor gene; mutations associated with aggressive tumors and poor prognosis (3)(6)
