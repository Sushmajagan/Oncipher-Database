Immunotherapy

Immunotherapy helps a person’s immune system fight cancer.  Your doctor may suggest biomarker tests to help predict your response to certain immunotherapy drugs.

Immunotherapy drugs used to treat squamous cell carcinoma of the lip and oral cavity cancer that has come back or spread to other parts of the body include:

1.nivolumab

Nivolumab works by binding to the protein PD-L1 on the surface of some cancer cells, which keeps cancer cells from suppressing the immune system. This allows the immune system to attack the cancer cells. Nivolumab is a type of immunotherapy drug called an immune checkpoint inhibitor.

2.pembrolizumab

Pembrolizumab works by binding to the protein PD-1 on the surface of certain immune cells called T cells, which keeps cancer cells from suppressing the immune system. This allows the immune system to attack the cancer cells. Pembrolizumab is a type of immunotherapy drug called an immune checkpoint inhibitor.Source
