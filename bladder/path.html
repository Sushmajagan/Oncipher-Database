Key Signaling Pathways in Bladder cancer

Bladder cancer pathogenesis involves dysregulation of multiple oncogenic signaling pathways that drive tumor progression, metastasis, and therapeutic resistance.

1. PI3K/AKT/mTOR Pathway

Dysregulated in >50% of cases, this pathway promotes:

Cell survival: AKT phosphorylation inhibits apoptosis via BCL-2 suppression and MDM2-mediated p53 degradation

Metabolic reprogramming: mTOR activation enhances glycolysis and protein synthesis, supporting rapid proliferation

Therapeutic resistance: PIK3CA mutations (20% prevalence) confer resistance to EGFR/HER2 inhibitors

2. Ras/Raf/MEK/MAPK Cascade

Dysregulated in >50% of NHLs via:

PTEN deletions (30% in T-cell lymphomas)

Activated by HRAS mutations (15-20% of cases) and EGFR/HER2 overexpression:

Induces EMT through Snail/Slug upregulation

Stimulates IL-6/IL-8 secretion to create pro-inflammatory microenvironments

Synergizes with PI3K/AKT via RTK crosstalk in muscle-invasive disease

3. Wnt/β-Catenin Signaling Aberrantly activated in 30-40% of tumors:

Nuclear β-catenin accumulation drives CCND1 and MYC transcription

Promotes cancer stemness through LGR5/LRP6 interactions

Correlates with cisplatin resistance (HR=2.1)

4. Androgen Receptor (AR) Pathway AR overexpression in 60% of urothelial carcinomas:

Enhances FGFR3 transcription, particularly in non-muscle-invasive tumors

Mediates chemoresistance via doxorubicin efflux pumps

Shows gender disparity with higher activity in male patients
