Key Signaling Pathways in Breast cancer

Breast cancer progression is driven by dysregulation of core oncogenic pathways, modulatory networks, and subtype-specific signaling cascades.

1. HER2/ERBB2 Signaling

Activation: ERBB2 amplification (15–20% of cases) drives ligand-independent dimerization, activating downstream effectors

Functional impact: Sustains proliferation via MAPK/ERK and PI3K/AKT.

Promotes metastasis through MMP-9/IL-6 secretion

HER2-low tumors exhibit mixed basal-luminal features with elevated AR and CLDN8

2. Estrogen Receptor (ER) Pathway

Mechanism:

Ligand-dependent ERα activation regulates transcription of CCND1 and MYC.

Resistance drivers:

ESR1 mutations (30% of metastatic cases) and crosstalk with CDK4/6

3. PI3K/AKT/mTOR Axis

Genetic alterations: PIK3CA mutations (40%) and PTEN loss (35%)

Oncogenic effects:AKT phosphorylates BAD/FOXO1, inhibiting apoptosis.

mTORC1 enhances HIF1α-mediated angiogenesis

4. Wnt/β-Catenin Signaling

Activation: CTNNB1 mutations or DKK1/FRP1 silencing in 30–40% of tumors

Role:Sustains BCSC self-renewal via LGR5 and promotes EMT through TWIST1

5. p53 Pathway

Alterations: Mutations in 20% of tumors; broader dysregulation in 60% via MDM2/MDM4

Impact:Loss of DNA repair capacity and apoptosis evasion

Modulatory Signaling Networks

TGF-β/SMAD Pathway

Dual role:Tumor-suppressive early; pro-metastatic late via SMAD2/3-mediated EMT and immunosuppressive TME

NF-κB Signaling

Activators:Inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α) and RTK crosstalk

Effects: Upregulates BCL2 and COX-2, linking inflammation to therapy resistance

Hedgehog (Hh) Signaling

Components: SMO mutations and GLI1/2 overexpression in BCSCs

Function: Maintains stemness via BMI1 and induces chemo-resistance through ABCG2/MGMT

JAK/STAT3 Axis

Activation:IL-6/gp130 signaling in TNBC.

Outcome:MYC stabilization and PD-L1 upregulation for immune evasion Source: (1) (2) (3) (4) (5)
