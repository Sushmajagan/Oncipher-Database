Key Signaling Pathways in Cervical cancer

Cervical cancer is primarily driven by persistent high-risk human papillomavirus (HPV) infection, whose oncoproteins (E5, E6, E7) disrupt multiple cellular signaling networks to promote malignant transformation.

1. PI3K/AKT/mTOR Pathway

Activation: Frequently upregulated by HPV E6/E7 oncoproteins, which promote phosphorylation of AKT and downstream mTOR signaling.

Consequences: Enhances cell survival, proliferation, migration, invasion, and resistance to apoptosis. Also supports epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT) and restricts cellular senescence, especially under hypoxic conditions

2. Wnt/β-Catenin Pathway

Activation:

E6/E6AP-mediated degradation of NHERF1 leads to β-catenin accumulation and nuclear translocation.

Consequences:

Induces transcription of oncogenic targets (e.g., c-Myc, cyclin D1, survivin), promoting proliferation, cell cycle progression, and inhibition of apoptosis

3. MAPK/ERK Pathway

Activation: HPV E5 stabilizes EGFR, enhancing downstream RAF/MEK/ERK signaling.

Consequences:Drives cell proliferation, survival, and transformation; also implicated in resistance to therapy and increased mitogenic signaling

4. Notch Signaling

Activation: HPV E6 interacts with MAML1, disrupting Notch-mediated differentiation and cell fate determination.

Consequences:Impairs normal epithelial differentiation, maintains cells in a proliferative state, and supports viral persistence and oncogenesis

5. Hedgehog (Hh) Pathway

Activation: Upregulated in cervical squamous cell carcinoma (SCC), contributing to tumor progression.

Consequences: Promotes cell proliferation, stemness, and survival

6. NF-κB Pathway

Activation:Modulated by HPV oncoproteins and inflammatory cytokines.

Consequences: Supports chronic inflammation, immune evasion, and survival signaling

7. JAK/STAT Pathway

Activators:Frequently upregulated, especially in SCC, through cytokine signaling (e.g., IL-6, interferon).

Effects: Promotes immune evasion, proliferation, and survival Source: (1) (2) (3) (4)
