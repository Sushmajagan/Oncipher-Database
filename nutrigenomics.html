Page Title Page Title
Nutrigenomic Factor Type Function Significance
Folate Vitamin/B Vitamin Involved in DNA synthesis and repair; methylation processes. Adequate intake may reduce breast cancer risk, particularly in women with BRCA mutations.
Genistein Isoflavone (Soy) Acts as a phytoestrogen; modulates estrogen receptor activity; influences tumor suppressor genes. Associated with reduced risk of breast cancer, especially in BRCA mutation carriers.
Lycopene Carotenoid Antioxidant properties; may inhibit cell proliferation and induce apoptosis. Linked to lower breast cancer risk; effective in reducing tumor growth in some studies.
Curcumin Polyphenol (Turmeric) Anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects; modulates multiple signaling pathways involved in cancer. Exhibits potential anti-cancer effects by inhibiting tumor growth and metastasis in preclinical studies.
Omega-3 Fatty Acids Fatty acids Anti-inflammatory properties; may influence gene expression related to inflammation and cancer progression. Associated with reduced breast cancer risk; beneficial effects on tumor growth regulation.
Vitamin D Vitamin Regulates calcium metabolism; influences cell growth and differentiation. Low levels are linked to increased breast cancer risk; supplementation may have protective effects.
Fiber Dietary Fiber Aids in digestion and may influence estrogen metabolism through gut microbiota interactions. High fiber intake is associated with a lower risk of breast cancer, possibly through estrogen modulation.
Cruciferous Vegetables Vegetables (e.g., broccoli) Contain glucosinolates that are converted to bioactive compounds with anti-cancer properties. Linked to reduced breast cancer risk due to their role in detoxification and hormone regulation.
Caffeine Stimulant May influence estrogen metabolism and has antioxidant properties Mixed results on breast cancer risk; some studies suggest a protective effect, while others show no association.
