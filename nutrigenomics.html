Phytonutrients Various Polyphenols Modulate gene expression, inhibit cell proliferation, and induce apoptosis. Potentially beneficial in preventing and treating thyroid cancer by targeting multiple molecular pathways. (1)
Calcium Various Calcium Acts as a cofactor in cellular processes, including mitosis and apoptosis. Essential for patients post-thyroidectomy to prevent osteoporosis; may influence thyroid cancer progression. (2)
Magnesium Various Magnesium Participates in over 300 biological reactions, including DNA replication and antioxidant defense. Magnesium deficiency may contribute to carcinogenesis; important for maintaining cellular integrity. (2)
Selenium GPX1, Selenoproteins Selenium Component of antioxidant enzymes that protect against oxidative stress. Exhibits chemopreventive effects against various cancers, including thyroid cancer; influences inflammatory markers. (2)
Resveratrol RARβ, PTEN Resveratrol Stimulates expression of tumor suppressor genes involved in cell cycle regulation. May inhibit oncogenic signaling pathways, thus having a protective effect against thyroid cancer development. (2)
Genistein ERα, ATM Genistein Acts as a phytoestrogen influencing various signaling pathways; promotes apoptosis and reduces cell viability. Associated with reduced risk of thyroid cancer through modulation of gene expression and epigenetic changes. (1)
Quercetin Various Quercetin Exhibits antioxidant properties and influences gene expression related to inflammation and apoptosis. May have protective effects against thyroid cancer by modulating inflammatory responses and cellular survival pathways. (1)
