Chemotherapy

Chemotherapy is a vital component in the treatment of cervical cancer, especially for cases that are locally advanced or have metastasized.

  • Chemotherapy is used to kill cancer cells throughout the body and is particularly effective for cervical cancer that has spread beyond the cervix or recurred after initial treatment.
  • It may also be administered after surgery to eliminate any remaining cancer cells or in conjunction with radiation therapy to enhance treatment efficacy.

Common Chemotherapy Regimens

Cisplatin:

 A platinum-based drug commonly used in combination with radiation.

Carboplatin:

 Similar to cisplatin, often used in various regimens.

Paclitaxel (Taxol):

 Used for its ability to inhibit cell division.

Topotecan:

 A topoisomerase inhibitor used for recurrent cases.

5-Fluorouracil (5-FU):

 An antimetabolite that interferes with DNA synthesis.

Bevacizumab (Avastin):

A monoclonal antibody that targets blood vessel formation, often added to chemotherapy regimens.

Chemotherapy can lead to a range of side effects, which may include:

1.Fatigue:

 A common experience during treatment.

2.Nausea and Vomiting:

 Patients often report feeling sick.

3.Weight Loss and Appetite Changes:

 Many experience a decrease in appetite and subsequent weight loss.

4.Hair Loss:

This is another frequent side effect due to the impact of chemotherapy on rapidly dividing cells Source
